Featured Works

We Reach
Celebrating the decades of images from the Hubble and James Webb telescopes, the poetry (by F. Taylor Atkinson) delves deep into humanity's unyielding quest to unravel the mysteries of the cosmos. Through soaring melodies and intricate harmonies, the piece paints a vivid portrait of our collective pursuit of understanding, punctuated by moments of triumph and revelation.

To the River!
"To the River" captures the poet’s deep contemplation—his passion for Old Alberto’s daughter entwined with the rhythmic pulse of the water. This evocative work beautifully portrays the timeless dance between passion and vulnerability, leaving audiences swept away by its heartfelt intensity.

Zuppa, Fichi, Bostregno
Set to the humorous poetry of 17th-century Italian poet Francesca Turina, this lively piece playfully bemoans a diet of soup and sweets, as the singer longs for the hearty satisfaction of meat before wasting away. Filled with clever word painting, spirited rhythms, and a touch of dramatic groans (literally), this piece is a begrudging ode to aging!

Bio

Bryan aims to engage audiences and reinforce core musical concepts for students by crafting high-quality and accessible concert music. His music features captivating harmonic textures, organic meters, and clear musical concepts.

Winner of the Ars Nova 7th Annual Colorado Composer's Competition, Bryan has been a featured composer in concerts across the U.S. and achieves regular performances of his works at large events, such as the state MEA (Music Educators Association) annual conferences. You can find his music with J.W. Pepper and Hal Leonard. Bryan earned his Master's of Music in Composition in 2020 from the University of Nebraska - Lincoln.
